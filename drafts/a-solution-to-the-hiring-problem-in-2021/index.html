Tech Hiring is Broken; Let's Try This Instead

Written by Jordan H.

It’s common knowledge that within the past decade there has been a growing trend of a tech worker shortage.

As a tech worker, I wholeheartedly disagree. Unless the earth really is flat, there is not a shortage of tech workers: it’s just employers suck at finding them.

My Own Experience

For months after I lost my last job I spent most of my time filling out pesky job applications. Spending 3 hours (or more) for every job application making sure everything was top-notch, all the dates aligned and I included just the right amount of keywords.

And then I’d throw the line into the ocean, hoping for a bite.

That was full-time work. Eventually I had to take an emergency retail job since money (and unemployment) ran out. So I was looking for work full time, working a 30hr/week retail job, and–oh, yeah–making sure my skills were up to date.

And networking. Because maybe I could find the right connection.

Unfortunately, networking didn’t result in any job. Not a single one.

I would literally be standing 3 feet from an HR person. We’d agree that I had the skills for a very open position. They could literally hire me right there; or at the very least, schedule an interview. I could start next week.
